# Synthesize evidence into Beamer

Treat the validated report bundle as the source for a cycle-specific slide
companion and `reports/discourse_jepa_neurips.tex` as the cumulative
paper-facing deck unless `research/STATE.md` names a replacement. Use only
supported evidence. Keep observed-intent, action-free, hard-text, and oracle
diagnostic claims visibly distinct.

Make slides teachable to a reader with almost no machine-learning background.
Use as many slides as clarity needs. Begin with ordinary-language motivation
and a concrete example before architecture or equations. Give each slide one
message, three to six complete-sentence bullets, and at most one readable
figure or table. Include “what to notice” and “why it matters” on result slides.
Define every specialized term. Label invalidated historical findings and never
promote them to headline evidence.

Compile twice, inspect the PDF visually, and check for overflow, clipped labels,
unreadable legends, and stale numbers. Do not change scientific claims merely
to improve the narrative.
